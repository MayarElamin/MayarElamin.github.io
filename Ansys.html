Advanced Flight Structure Analysis

This project focused on analysing the structural performance of a flight structure using Finite Element Analysis in ANSYS Mechanical APDL under loading and dynamic conditions. Our team analyzed stress distribution, shear stresses, buckling behavior, and vibration behavior to identify potential failure modes and assess the overall stability and integrity. Through stress contour plots, buckling mode shapes, and modal analysis at critical locations such as the wing tip and mid-span, we learned how the structure behaves under load. This project helped us understand better how simulation results could affect engineering decisions. This strengthened my experience in structural analysis, finite element modeling, and collaborative problem-solving.

My Role

  • Built the finite element model in ANSYS Mechanical APDL, including material properties, boundary conditions, and loading cases.
  • Conducted stress and shear analyses to identify critical load paths and high-stress regions.
  • Performed buckling analysis to evaluate structural stability and determine the first three buckling modes.
  • Performed modal and frequency-response analyses to analyze vibration behavior.
  • Analyzed results at key locations, including the wing tip and mid-span, to understand worst-case structural responses.
  • Collaborated with team members to validate assumptions, review results, and align analysis with project objectives.
  • Documented methodology and results in a technical report.
