Formula SAE

Using SolidWorks, this team project involved CAD modeling the Boeing 737-100, the first aircraft in Boeing’s iconic 737 family. The 737-100 was chosen because it represents a foundational commercial aircraft design, which was both simple enough to model at a student level, and historically significant. The seating capacity of the 737-100 is up to 118 passengers, hence, it was used for short and medium-range trips with Lufthansa purchasing 22 of the 30 units produced.

The goal of the project was to create a complete, proportionally accurate SolidWorks model and develop core CAD skills. This was my first official SolidWorks aircraft project, and I was responsible for designing the fuselage, which was both the most challenging and rewarding part of the build. With limited reference material, I translated top, side, and front views into a three-dimensional model using splines, lofts, and feature cuts.

Throughout the project, our team collaborated to resolve modeling issues and ensure consistency across the final design. The project strengthened my skills in CAD modeling, problem-solving, engineering judgment, and technical communication, and also provided hands-on experience with the kind of iterative design process used in industry.

My Role

  • Led the fuselage design and modeling of a Boeing 737-100 in SolidWorks.
  • Incorporated real-world aircraft standards, including Boeing passenger window dimensions, to ensure realistic scaling and design accuracy.
  • Applied engineering judgment to estimate cockpit dimensions using reference images where formal drawings were unavailable
  • Troubleshot design challenges by collaborating with teammates and seeking information from other teams.
  • Contributed to a collaborative team environment, communicating design decisions clearly and adapting based on group feedback
