Senior Design Project

As aerospace engineering seniors, our capstone project challenges us to design and build an RC aircraft with mission requirements that change each year. This year’s objective focuses on developing a small unmanned aerial system (sUAS) capable of supporting Department of Defense operations. As part of team Pallo, we designed Pallo’s Iron Eagle, which is a fixed-wing sUAS designed to carry variable payloads up to 1.5 lbs with a 30-minute flight endurance. The aircraft's design includes a detachable wing, with a lifting canard for enhanced aerodynamic efficiency, and a sub-five-second payload swap system as the requirements given mention.

My Role

  • Collaborated with teammates to design and model an RC aircraft with a hot-swappable payload drop system.
  • Contributed to the design of some components, including wings and landing gear, using SolidWorks.
  • Evaluated alternative manufacturing methods and material options to balance performance, cost, and design constraints.
  • Coordinated the manufacturing timeline to maintain progress and meet project deadlines.
  • Tracked inventory, issued manufacturing orders, and developed a detailed bill of materials (BOM).
  • Assisted with wind tunnel testing and contributed to data collection, test execution, and post-test analysis.
