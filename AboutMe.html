A Journey from Muscat to the Skies

Hi, my name is Mayar. Welcome to my portfolio!

Born in Chicago and raised in Muscat, Oman, I’ve grown up between two cultures that taught me adaptability, curiosity, and the ability to navigate new environments. After earning my Associate of Science at Rock Valley College, the pandemic, like for many, brought uncertainty. During that time, my sister and I launched a home-based floral business in Rockford, IL, despite having no prior experience, only a shared love of florals and design. Managing everything from logistics and design concepts to customer communication taught me resilience, resourcefulness, and how to learn quickly, which are skills that have stayed with me far beyond entrepreneurship.

Today, I bring that same energy into my aerospace engineering journey at Iowa State University. I’ve built experience in aerodynamics, CFD, composite manufacturing, MDO research, RC aircraft design, and flight testing through hands-on projects such as Formula SAE, AIAA Design-Build-Fly, and undergraduate research under Dr. Ping He. Throughout my journey at ISU, I have had the opportunity to work on optimizing a wing’s geometry, analyze surrogate models in SMT, produce carbon-fiber components for Cyclone Racing, and much more!

Beyond technical work, I have taken on leadership roles that strengthened my teamwork, communication, and operations planning skills. I have led documentation and test-flight coordination for DBF, supported student organizations, and taught Arabic to young learners. Music also remains a grounding part of my life as well as I played the organ and xylophone, and grew an interest in tango during my time at ISU.

I am graduating in December 2025 and I am excited to bring my blend of technical skils, creativity, hands-on engineering experience, and global perspective to an engineering team that values curiosity and innovation. I’m always motivated by challenges, and enjoy collaboration, whether in the classroom, the lab, or in industry.

Senior Design

Pallo’s Iron Eagle is a fixed-wing small unmanned aerial system (sUAS) designed for the Department of Defense to carry variable payloads up to 1.5 lbs with a 30-minute flight endurance. The aircraft features a detachable wing design, a lifting canard for improved efficiency, and a sub-five-second payload swap system for rapid mission adaptability.

Formula SAE

As part of Iowa State University’s Formula SAE team, the aerodynamics subsystem focuses on designing and manufacturing aerodynamic components that improve downforce and vehicle performance. The work includes CFD analysis, carbon fiber fabrication, and side wing design optimization to improve the car’s lift-to-drag ratio and overall efficiency. This project combines hands-on manufacturing with advanced simulation and design to push the limits of a student-built race car.

November 2024

High Altitude Balloon Experiments
in Technology

This project investigated how sound propagates at high altitudes by launching an experiment to the edge of space with the Iowa State HABET team. Using an Adafruit CLUE board, the system measured sound amplitude relative to atmospheric pressure throughout the balloon’s ascent. The collected data confirmed that sound intensity decreases with altitude due to reduced air density.

June 2024

Aerodynamic Shape Optimization Research

This research focused on aerodynamic optimization of aircraft wings using Multidisciplinary Design Optimization (MDO) methods. Machine learning and reduced-order modeling techniques were applied through the SMT toolbox to improve aerodynamic efficiency. Kriging and Radial Basis Function (RBF) surrogate models were utilized to accelerate design evaluations and enhance the accuracy of aerodynamic predictions.
