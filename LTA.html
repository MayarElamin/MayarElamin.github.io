Lighter Than Air (LTA)

The Catbus project was my introduction to aerospace design and served as a foundation for understanding key aerodynamic principles in a practical setting. As a team of four, we were tasked with designing and constructing a lighter-than-air RC aircraft. Our team aimed to achieve stable, efficient flight that was assissted with agiant helium balloon through a challenging obstacle course. The team decided to draw inspiration from My Neighbor Totoro’s Catbus. The design integrated both the aesthetics and aerodynamic functionality.

As the team’s manufacturing and budgeting engineer, I managed material selection, inventory, and cost control to ensure we remained within budget while meeting structural and performance goals. The aircraft featured a tilted bi-copter configuration with dual propellers that provided balanced lift and an auxiliary motor generating forward thrust. This setup allowed us to explore the optimization of thrust-to-drag, stability control, and aerodynamic flow.

By the end of the semester, Catbus became more than just a creative design, it was a hands-on experiment in how aerodynamics, propulsion, and engineering decisions interact to create successful flight. The final competition brought together all teams in the course to test our design’s performance under real-world flight conditions.

My Role

  • Managed inventory, budgeting, and material allocation to stay within financial constraints.
  • Collaborated with the chief design and manufacturing engineers to integrate aerodynamic and structural requirements into the final design.
  • Developed a balanced propulsion configuration with dual lift propellers for stability and a rear propeller for thrust generation.
  • Applied aerodynamic principles to minimize turbulence and optimize thrust-to-drag performance for smoother flight.
  • Contributed to the aesthetic integration of the My Neighbor Totoro Catbus character by incorporating its design elements while maintaining structural integrity and weight efficiency.
  • Designed functional landing gear inspired by the Catbus legs to enhance stability during takeoff and landing.
