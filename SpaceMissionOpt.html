Space Mission Optimization

This project was part of the AERE 463 course (Introduction to Multidisciplinary Design Optimization). Using Python language, the project applies MDO techniques learnt thoughout the semester to the planning of an interplanetary mission from Earth to Europa. Our Team, MDOrbit, was motivated by the growing scientific interest in Europa’s subsurface ocean and the high cost of deep-space exploration. The final study aimed to minimize required Δv, which is the total velocity change required to execute a space mission directly affecting fuel mass and launch cost, and launch mass through optimal rocket staging, interplanetary transfers, and gravity-assist strategies using Mars and Jupiter flybys. My team was able to optimize trajectory parameters, staging masses, and time-of-flight under constraints. The results demonstrated how MDO can significantly improve mission efficiency and ultimately reduce fuel costs.

My Role

  • Developed XDSM diagrams to identify the different subsystems in the optimization framework
  • Contributed to the rocket staging optimization script.
  • Documented and commented code to improve its clarity, usability, and collaboration
